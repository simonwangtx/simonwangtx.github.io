DMS (Dynamic Moving Storm)

January 01, 1970


Dr. Fang’s research team developed a hydro-meteorological model called Dynamic Moving Storm (DMS) which shows the movement and rain intensity of storms in real time. DMS connects to rainfall runoff models for hydrologic analyses to show variations in rain patterns. Using DMS has a significant advantage over a stationary storm design since storm movement greatly affects distributed rainfall and resulting runoff. In addition, Some of the most important features of DMS have been tested in different situations to quantify and better understand the associated hydrologic effects.
Current efforts, such as the IKE reconstruction trial, validate how well DMS can recreate historical events. Future research directions for DMS include ensemble forecast, storm structure study, and storm motion tracking.
